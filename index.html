Example
*Example input

Context: Instagram feed ad for Vela, a sustainable sneaker brand targeting women 25–35. Goal: stop the scroll and drive product page clicks.

She doesn't follow trends. She sets them. Meet Vela — the sneaker built for women who move on their own terms. Recycled ocean plastic uppers. Zero dye process. 100% carbon neutral shipping. Yours for $148. Free returns, always. [Shop Vela →]

The Stranger
01Top of Funnel

"The opener almost got me — but I wasn't sure this was about shoes until halfway through."

What's working
  • "She doesn't follow trends" creates an identity hook — you read it and feel seen
  • "Zero dye process" is specific and unusual — more interesting than just "sustainable"
  • Price and free returns up front remove hesitation early
What's not landing
  • What does Vela actually look like? I'm picturing a shoe with nothing to grab onto
  • Is "ocean plastic" a feature or am I supposed to feel guilty?
  • The "[Shop Vela →]" bracket format reads like placeholder copy
Fix before tomorrow

Add one concrete visual detail — a color, a silhouette word — something that makes me picture the shoe in the first 3 seconds.

The Skeptic
02Mid Funnel

"This reads like a brand that hired someone good to write for them — but I still don't know who's actually behind it."

What's working
  • "Zero dye process" is a genuine differentiator — I'd Google that
  • "100% carbon neutral shipping" is auditable — earns trust rather than demands it
  • Free returns signals real confidence in the product
What's not landing
  • "Move on their own terms" does a lot of lifting without backup — what does it mean for this shoe?
  • Why ocean plastic? Is there a story, or just a material choice?
  • Every sustainable sneaker says "built for women who..." — what makes Vela different?
Fix before tomorrow

Give the brand a real point of view in one sentence — what does Vela believe that other sneaker brands don't?

The Converter
03Bottom of Funnel

"I'm interested and the price is reasonable — but I don't know where I'm actually going when I click."

What's working
  • $148 with free returns is a reasonable ask — the value math lands
  • Return policy addresses my biggest hesitation before I had to ask
  • CTA is at the end where I'm most ready to act — right placement
What's not landing
  • When I click "Shop Vela," do I land on the product or a homepage?
  • Is there one style, one colorway? If I'm choosing, help me choose
  • Why now? Free returns are always available — what's my reason to act today?
Fix before tomorrow

Rewrite the CTA to name the destination: "See the Vela in Chalk →" or "Find your size →" — something that tells me exactly where I'm landing.
